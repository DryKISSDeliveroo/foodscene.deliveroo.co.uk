Top 5 eateries for the best Indian street food

  • 22 July 2017
  • Editor
  • Read time: 3 min
indian street food chaat
Share this article

As one of the most popular cuisines in the country, Indian food will always remain at the top of the chart when it comes to takeaways in the UK. And for the most authentic, awesome Indian-inspired eats, it doesn't get better than classic street food. So if you're a huge fan of spicy foods and want to know some of the best places around for authentic, Indian street fare, look no further…

1. Tandoori Subzi, Tamarind Kitchen

A post shared by Tamarind Kitchen (@tamarindkitchenlondon) on May 3, 2017 at 9:54am PDT

The dishes you'll find served at Tamarind Kitchen are practically bursting with all the South Asian herbs and spices that make street food from this area so special. We recommend their tasty Tandoori Subzi – delicate paneer paired with mushrooms and broccoli and grilled with hot red chillies and cumin. And the guys behind the magic here are also responsible for the award-winning sister-restaurant Tamari, so you know they're the real deal.

Where: Tamarind Kitchen, London

2. Paneer and Mushroom Tikka, Bundobust

A post shared by Bundobust (@bundobust) on Apr 1, 2017 at 3:21am PDT

Feeling disenchanted with meagre vegetarian options you find? Don't worry, Bundobust specialises in whipping up some awesome vegetarian curries. While their Paneer and Mushroom Tikka has proved to be one of the most popular dishes on the menu, it's their crispy bhajis seasoned with fragrant rose and served in a rich tomato sauce that we can't get enough of. And don't even get us started on their Bundo Chaat, one forkful and you'll be hooked.

Where: Bundobust, Leeds

3. Chicken Achari, Tuk Tuk

A post shared by Tuk Tuk Indian Street Food (@tuktukstreetuk) on Mar 9, 2016 at 7:55am PST

They usually serve up their goodies stacked in Mumbai-style tiffin lunch boxes, and although you won't get them when you order in, the food is so good we don't think you'll mind. While vegetarians will love their protein-packed Tarka Dahl made from yellow and black lentils, their Chicken Achari is one of their newer dishes this year, with a blend of lime, pickle and masala spices.

Where: Tuk Tuk, Glasgow

4. Chili Chicken wrap, Tifinbox

A post shared by London Cheap Eats (@ldncheapeats) on Feb 3, 2017 at 4:16am PST

If you're stuck in the office in need of a fiery treat, Tifinbox is there to answer your prayers and cure your cravings. For lunch, we'd recommend one of their curry wraps, packing in all of the heat of traditional Indian dishes, from chicken tikka to minced lamb kebabs, into one handy burrito-style wrap. We love the Chili Chicken number, a Indo-Chinese dish of marinated chicken cooked up with garlic, ginger, chillies, soy sauce, peppers and onions, bundled up with salad in a soft wrap.

Where: Tifinbox, London

5. Tandoori Mixed Grill, The Cat's Pyjamas

A post shared by Caitlin ✨ (@caitlin_marsh) on Mar 11, 2016 at 11:45am PST

But if you're a carnivore through and through, the succulent meats on offer as part of the Tandoori Mixed Grill at the Cat's Pyjamas is a must-order dish for you. Succulent malai tikka, sweet seekh kebabs and fresh prawns served with a refreshing mint chutney – it's the perfect takeaway treat, especially when you add in a homemade garlic naan.

Where: The Cat's Pyjamas, two locations in Leeds
