10 of the most ordered Deliveroo dishes in London

  • 19 January 2017
  • Becky Hughes
  • Read time: 4 min
london most popular best dishes
Share this article

We've all got our Deliveroo favourites. Whether your go-to is a chorizo-topped pizza from that Neapolitan joint at the end of your road, or you just can't resist wagamama's Chilli Squid delivered to your desk, there's always that one dish that's sure to make your day.

Gourmet burgers were the favourite food delivery of Deliveroo customers last year, with Mexican and Japanese menu items notching up some of the most orders across the UK.

London's favourite Deliveroo dishes

Turns out the capital loves getting gourmet burgers delivered too – and chicken. Especially Korean fried chicken. Here are ten of London's most popular Deliveroo dishes.

1.Dead Hippie, MEATliquor

A post shared by MEATliquor Bars & Diners. (@meatgram) on Nov 16, 2016 at 2:56am PST

This burger (almost) needs no introduction. MEATliquor's signature Dead Hippie – French mustard-fried beef patties smothered in a secret sauce – is now a cult favourite.

Where: MEATliquor W1, MEATliquor N1, MEATliquor Croydon, MEATmission, MEATmarket

2. Sloppy Joe Dog, Bubbledogs

A post shared by bubbledogsuk (@bubbledogsuk) on Nov 21, 2016 at 8:53am PST

The geniuses at Bubbledogs dreamt of pairing champagne with the humble hot dog, and it just works. Their Sloppy Joe Dog teams a gourmet sausage with beef chilli, cheddar and onions – and Londoners love it.

Where: Bubbledogs, Fitzrovia

3. Korean Fried Chicken Wings, Bone Daddies

A post shared by Bone Daddies (@bonedaddies) on Mar 5, 2016 at 8:17am PST

They might be most famous for their ramen, but it's Bone Daddies' Korean Fried Chicken Wings that Deliveroo's London-based customers find most irresistible. Marinated in ginger and white pepper, then coated in a sansho pepper flour, they've nailed the recipe to seductively crispy chicken.

Where: Bone Daddies Kensington, Bone Daddies Old Street, Bone Daddies Soho, Bone Daddies Bermondsey

4. Yangum Chicken, On The Bab

A post shared by On The Bab (@onthebab) on Apr 9, 2015 at 6:18am PDT

More Korean fried chicken on the menu – this time, from Shoreditch's On the Bab. Served with either a soy ginger or a sweet and spicy sauce, plus crushed peanuts, they always hit the spot.

Where: On The Bab, Shoreditch

5. Tribute, Honest Burgers

A post shared by Honest Burgers (@honestburgers) on Jan 7, 2017 at 2:45am PST

Paying tribute to the classic US-style burger (geddit?), this is Honest Burgers' answer to MEATliquor's Dead Hippie – with a rasher of bacon and rosemary salted chips for good measure.  

Where: Various locations, London

6. Green Chicken Curry, Busaba

A post shared by Busaba Bangkok Thai (@busababangkokthai) on May 19, 2015 at 12:02pm PDT

As well as their signature calamari, one of Busaba's most loved dishes is their Green Chicken Curry. Packing more of a punch than your average Thai green, you'll find grilled chicken, pea, aubergine and corn in a moreish curry sauce.

Where: Various locations, UK

7. Pad Thai, Papaya Tree

A post shared by 209 Kensington High St, London (@papaya_tree_thai_restaurant_w8) on Feb 25, 2016 at 7:00am PST

Serving Thai food since the 60s, and using fresh ingredients imported from Thailand twice a week, Kensington's Papaya Tree has mastered the art of the perfect Pad Thai. Fried rice noodles with egg and beansprouts, finished off with ground peanuts and a wedge of lime.

Where: Papaya Tree, Kensington

8. Chicken Katsu Curry, wagamama

A post shared by wagamama_uk (@wagamama_uk) on Aug 9, 2016 at 7:49am PDT

We can credit wagamama for putting katsu curry firmly on the UK's culinary map. And who can resist that aromatic katsu curry sauce? Not London, that's for sure - the wagamama staple is one of the capital's favourites.

Where: Various locations, UK

9. Margherita, Rossopomodoro

A post shared by ROSSOPOMODOROUK (@rossopomodorouk) on Jun 26, 2016 at 6:05am PDT

It's no news that Londoners love pizza – but their favourite kind? A classic Neapolitan-style margherita from high street pizza heros, Rossopomodoro. Authenticity is what they're all about – everything is sourced from Naples, from the ingredients used to the chefs themselves.

Where: Various locations, UK

10. Spicy Tuna Maki, Yoobi

A post shared by yoobisushi (@yoobisushi) on Feb 19, 2016 at 11:17am PST

Yoobi's Japanese-Brazilian cuisine has taken London by storm, with several new sites popping up last year. These sushi pros specialise in the temaki, but it's the Spicy Tuna Maki rolls that have racked up the most orders.

Where: Yoobi Soho, Yoobi Battersea
